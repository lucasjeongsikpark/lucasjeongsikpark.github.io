Jeongsik Park

Computer Science MS student at the University of Southern California.

I believe that, in the future of real-world scenarios, the focus should shift toward smaller models that excel at specific tasks, rather than massive, multi-task models (or making multi-task models smaller). This approach can lead to 1) improved performance, 2) reduced inference costs, and 3) faster training times. To achieve this, we require not only amazing model architectures but also meticulously curated data crafted through appropriate methodologies for effective model training.

Therefore, my primary interests lie in:
1) On-Device (Small) Language Models, 2) Personalized AI Agents,
3) Large Language Models (LLMs), 4) Model Evaluation Systems,
5) Natural Language Processing, Multimodal AI, and Machine Learning. .

Before attending USC, I earned my undergraduate degree in Computer Science from the University of Texas at Dallas, where I conducted research in NLP (particularly focusing on data, LLMs, and multimodal systems) under the guidance of Dr. Vincent Ng. More details about my research can be found in my CV.
